The Academic Journey

A BSW curriculum blends liberal arts with professional training, culminating in a mandatory, transformative field experience.

What You'll Study: Core Curriculum

Foundations of Social Work

Explore the history, values, and ethical principles of the profession. Courses: Intro to Social Work, Social Welfare Policy.

Human Behavior (HBSE)

Understand human development across the lifespan within the context of social environments.

Practice Methods

Learn direct skills for working with individuals, families, groups, and communities.

Research & Evaluation

Develop skills to apply evidence-based practices and evaluate the effectiveness of interventions.

Diversity & Social Justice

Learn to work effectively with diverse populations and advocate for systemic change.

Fieldwork: 400+ Hours

The "signature pedagogy" where you apply classroom knowledge in a real-world agency setting.

How BSW Compares to Related Degrees

A BSW is a professional degree that qualifies you for practice right after graduation, a key difference from other social science majors.
